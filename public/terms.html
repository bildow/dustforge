5. Escrow

5.1 Nature of Escrow

The Escrow facility permits account holders to create DD-collateralized holds between two or more accounts with defined release, refund, and dispute flows. Escrow is a programmatic convenience for parties transacting on the Service and is not an act of money transmission, banking, trust services, or fiduciary agency by AKStrapped.

5.2 Creation and Funding

The creator of an Escrow funds the hold from their DD balance. Once funded, DD is reserved against the Escrow and is not usable for other purposes until released, refunded, or disputed per Sections 5.3–5.5.

5.3 Release

The creator of an Escrow may release funds to the designated beneficiary, subject to any conditions (“barrel gates”) encoded at creation. Release transfers the underlying DD to the beneficiary’s wallet and closes the Escrow.

5.4 Refund

Either the creator or the counterparty, as permitted by the Escrow’s configuration, may refund DD to the creator. Refund closes the Escrow.

5.5 Dispute

Either party may mark an Escrow as disputed. Marking an Escrow as disputed freezes movement of the underlying DD but does not itself award funds to either party. Disputes are a signal and record, not an adjudication. AKStrapped does not arbitrate Escrow disputes. Parties are expected to resolve disputes between themselves and to then use the release or refund flow to close the Escrow. If an Escrow remains disputed and unresolved for more than one hundred eighty (180) days, AKStrapped may, in its sole discretion, return the held DD to the creator, split the DD between the parties, or hold the DD indefinitely pending resolution; none of these options is a fiduciary commitment.

5.6 Limitations

You acknowledge that Escrow does not make AKStrapped a party to the underlying transaction, does not guarantee performance by any counterparty, and does not convert DD into a stored-value instrument usable outside the Service. AKStrapped is not liable for losses arising from counterparty non-performance, mistaken release, or refund by the parties.

Each secret is bound to one or more approved contexts (action type, target host, header or field, and similar parameters). Use-Tokens are typically valid for thirty (30) seconds and for a single redemption. Token parameters may change; current values are published in Service documentation.

6.5 The Rowen Bonded Courier

For higher-assurance operations, DemiPass may route secret use through the Rowen Bonded Courier, a clean-room intermediary that mediates chain of custody between the depositor, the DemiVault, and the downstream target. Rowen operates a dual-barrel architecture in which secret material and authorization are held by separate systems and assembled only at the moment of use, with automatic destruction within thirty (30) seconds. Rowen provides process boundary separation and auditable custody. Rowen does not provide hardware-equivalent isolation, and the Service does not claim that it does. You should size your trust accordingly.
